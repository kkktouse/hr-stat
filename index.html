Recruitment Performance Report

Period: 1 June 2025 – 13 August 2025

Executive Summary

This report summarizes the recruitment activities from June 1 to August 13, 2025. During this period, the team processed nearly 1,200 candidates and conducted 412 video screenings, resulting in 44 hires. A significant challenge identified is the high early-stage attrition, with 43% of new hires leaving the company, primarily within the first week and concentrated in the Lead Generation and Design departments. July recorded the highest attrition rate. Despite the high workload and turnover, the recruitment team has demonstrated substantial productivity, and there have been successful internal role transfers, indicating potential for internal mobility programs.

3. Key Insights and Trends

High Early-Stage Turnover

A total of 18 hires left or were terminated during the period, representing an attrition rate of 43% of all joined hires (18 out of 42). This turnover is particularly high within the first week of employment. The Lead Generation and UI/UX Design departments are most affected by this trend.

Peak Attrition in July

July saw the highest number of departures, accounting for 10 of the 18 total exits (56%). This concentration suggests a potential need to review the onboarding process or role expectations for cohorts hired during this month.

Intensive Recruitment Workload

The team managed a substantial pipeline, processing 1,194 candidates and conducting 412 video screenings in just over two months. This high volume underscores the significant effort required to achieve hiring targets.

Successful Internal Mobility

The successful transition of Elvira Morozova from a LeadGen role to a Recruiter position highlights a positive trend. This demonstrates an opportunity for developing internal talent and creating career progression paths within the company, which could also help improve retention.
